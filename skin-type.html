DRY SKIN

If it’s…tight, flaky, or uncomfortable, you likely have DRY skin

"Dry skin means that your face can't protect itself as well as it should from the environment. When the skin is dry, it develops tiny cracks in the outer layer, interfering with its ability to maintain hydration properly," says dermatologist Joshua Zeichner. As such, when you have dry skin choosing the right products is incredibly important. But surprisingly, the ones you should be choosing may go against your natural instincts. Moisture is key right?? But what if some “moisturising” products actually begot dryness? Are you shocked now? Read on for some tips and tricks as well as helpful products for that pesky dryness.

01 Start the Day Off Right Your morning prep is important to get your skin ready for the day! Individuals with dry skin types should aim to hydrate and protect their skin. 02 Choose a Non-Foaming Cleanser If there's one thing that hasn't changed about our skincare routines since childhood, it's starting the day with a fresh face—literally. Look for a gentle, non-foaming cleanser specifically made with hydrating ingredients. These products remove dirt and oil without disrupting the outer skin layer the way that some foaming cleansers do.03 Choose a Light Moisturizer When it comes to moisturizer shopping, look for light moisturizers that contain ingredients that can soften rough cells, pull in hydration, and form a protective seal over the surface of the skin such as hylauronic acid.04 Protect Yourself with SPF Look for sunscreens that, of course, help the skin lock in moisture. Rather than a straight sunscreen that you would use for the beach if you have dry skin, look for a moisturizer/sunscreen combo. Look for an SPF that is 30 or higher.

COMBINATION SKIN

If it’s…oily in some places, tight in others, you likely have COMBINATION skin.

Combination skin overproduces oil in some places and under-produces oil in others, giving you the most-frustrating sides of both worlds. But—and I’m about to blow your mind, here—you don’t have to use the same skin-care product over your entire face. WHAAAT. Yup! You can build a routine that utilizes oils and creams on your cheeks and jaw, for example, with lightweight serums and acne-treatments on just your forehead and nose, targeting each “problem area” with the correct products.

01 Cleansers can change with the seasons The right cleanser depends on your unique type of combination skin, you may have more oiliness or more dryness as the seasons change. In general, look for a gentle cleanser and wash your face with lukewarm water both morning and night.02 Tone (Morning & Night) A toner is essential for combination skin. Look for formulas designed to balance the skin—a safe bet is one that is both pH balanced and has hyaluronic acid, which will provide a much-needed boost of moisture but also help calm irritated skin.03 Choose the right Moisturizer Moisturizing is one of the most important steps for combination skin. Use a lightweight moisturizer in the morning and apply after cleansing and toning. At night, use a richer option that will renew skin while you sleep. Search for products with hyaluronic acid to help skin retain moisture.04 Protect Yourself with SPF Look for sunscreens that contain zinc oxide, this is a great ingredient for combination skin. Rather than a straight sunscreen that you would use for the beach if you have dry skin, look for a moisturizer/sunscreen combo. Look for an SPF that is 30 or higher.

OILY SKIN

If it’s…oily or shiny all over, you likely have OILY skin.

Oily skin overproduces sebum, and while that has its advantages (hello, fewer forehead wrinkles), it also has major drawbacks—namely looking and feeling oily. And as annoying as that can be for makeup, which tends to slip and slide on oily skin, it can also contribute to more acne and clogged pores, which means your routine should focus on regulating your sebum with light, water-based products, mattifying powders, and, if necessary, anti-acne treatments, all of which means…

01 Stay away from these products Since your oily skin is already producing too much of the good stuff, make sure to skip: Heavy facial oils, thick, oil-based creams , harsh scrubs or super-drying formulas, like charcoal masks and benzoyl peroxide (they’ll strip your skin, causing it to produce even more oil to compensate). 02 Choose specific indgredients in your Cleanser Use a gentle cleanser. If you need something stronger, look for ingredients specifically made for oily skin and acne, such as benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, glycolic acid, or beta-hydroxy acid. Although usually marketed for acne, these products work well for oily skin too. 03 Choose a Gel based Moisturizer When it comes to moisturizer shopping, look for gel based moisturizers that are free of oils and release specific ingredients that attract water from deeper layers of skin and bring it to the skin’s surface. 04 Protect Yourself with SPF Opt for an SPF that is free of oil. Rather than a straight sunscreen that you would use for the beach. Look for an SPF that is 30 or higher.

NORMAL SKIN

If it’s…pretty comfortable and not greasy, you likely have NORMAL skin.

Welp, your skin produces just the right amount of sebum all over your face. Congratulations; you can now have a party with the eight other people in the world who also have normal skin. I’m sure you’ll all be very happy together. No, I’m not bitter. Since your skin can handle both dry- and oily-type products, you should instead build your routine around specific skin concerns you have, like aging, redness, or dullness.
