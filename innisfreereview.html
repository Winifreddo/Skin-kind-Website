'INNIS-FREE' Nail Polish Review

Reviewed by Leigh DEC 2022

Verdict:

I'm a huge fan of Innis-Free and the products they have to offer but I must say I was sceptical about their nail polish range. It is true the ingredients are better for your nails than the traditional nail polishes you find on the shelf but a predominantly skin-care focused brand making nail polish? ehhhh. The brand claims it's a highly pigmented color which gives rich and intense colors to the nails in a single coat. It also claims its long-lasting to ensure long wear of vivid nail colors and finally there are triple-care ingredients for the health of nails consisting of a combination of calcium, argan oil and tangerine oil to provide nourishment to damaged nails.

About the brand

Innis-Free are a leading brand in sustainability and clean formulas. They've paved the way and revolutionised more ecofriendly products and packaging with their use of clean formulas and recycling initiatives.. The list goes on so head over to their page to read about their amazing climate efforts. They pride themselves on their natural ingredients derived from the island of Jeju and carved the idea of naturalism phrased as 'Natural Benefit from JEJU'. The brand discovered 12 ingredients from the island’s land, sea, water, trees and flowers. and their devotion to good quality ingredients represents the brand’s philosophy, which aims to present customers with a healthy form of beauty through natural products.

How to use

  • Ensure nails are clean and free of any other nail polish
  • Apply a base coat and wait to dry
  • Apply the nail polish and wait to dry
  • Optional: add a protective top coat

First Impressions

The polishes glide on nicely, although their claim that one coat is sufficient is not accurate. I found the first coat to be patchy and not very pigmented. (I had used a clear base coat from another brand). I needed three coats. The polish did dry qucikly which is great if your in a time pinch.

The colour was nice and they have a vast array to choose from however I found that it chipped very quickly. Less than a day after I applied it. I wasn't very impressed by this but tried again and unfortunately the same thing happened. For me I'd say its a good product if you just want the colour for one evening but the longevity of it (which it claims is very long-lasting) is not there. One last thought on the ingredients, the brand states there is a combination of calcium, argon oil and tangerine oil. They are absolutely correct however they are right at the bottom of the ingredients list meaning they are the ingredients that have the least concentration in the formula. So bear that in mind if you do decide to buy.

Ingredients

Butyl Acetate, Ethyl Acetate, Nitrocellulose, Adipic Acid/Neopentyl Glycol/Trimellitic Anhydride Copolymer, Acetyl Tributyl Citrate, Isopropyl Alcohol, Styrene/Acrylates Copolymer, Acrylates Copolymer, Stearalkonium Bentonite, Sucrose Acetate Isobutyrate, Silica Dimethyl Silylate, Titanium Dioxide (CI 77891), Dipropylene Glycol Dibenzoate, Limonene, Octocrylene, Polyvinyl Butyral, CI 77499/Iron Oxide Black, Citric Acid, CI 19140/Tartrazine, Fragrance, Dimethicone, CI 15850/Lithol Rubine B, Argania Spinosa Kernel Oil, Citrus Unshiu Fruit Oil, Butyl Alcohol, p Trimethylsiloxysilicate, Stearalkonium Hectorite, Linalool, Citral, Calcium Pantothenate
