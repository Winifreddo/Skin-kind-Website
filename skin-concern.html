PIGMENTATION

If you have…dark spots, or discoloration, or uncomfortable, you likely have Pigmentation.

Pigmentation refers to the coloring of the skin. Melanin is made by our skin cells and is the reason your skin has colour. With hyperpigmentation certain areas are darker than others, this doesn’t just affect the face, it can be present throughout the body also. But lets focus on the face, hyperpigmentation is a fairly common skin concern, you are not alone in the wars of your skin! there are many a skin enthusiat in the trenches with you. Lets level here, to completely remove signs of pigmentation is very difficult, we are fighting our skins natural make-up, but i’m here to tell you with the right routine and products you can reduce the discoloration pigmentation brings.

01 What Causes Hyperpigmentation? Sun damage- Eeek, those pesky UV rays. The sun can cause hyperpigmentation in the form of age spots. Chloasma (Melasma)- Patches of discoloration can be a side effect of hormonal changes. Post-inflammation- When skin goes through an iflammation (think acne/eczema) it can go into overdrive and produce darker spots as the healing process comes to an end. Medical conditions- Hyperpigmentation can be a symptom of an underlying medical condition such as Addison’s disease, if you are ever uncertain in these circumstances it is best to seek medical advice from your doctor or dermatologist. 02 How can you prevent hyperpigmentation Avoid direct sunlight- Hours between 10am-2pm can be detremental to the condition of your skin as these arethe peak hours for the sun and its darn UV. If you are out use a hat to provide shade for your face. Vitamin C- Vit C, is a game changer guys. With its antioxidising properties, it is known for its powerful skin-brightening and anti-ageing super powers that help to neutralise and limit sun damage. Apply SPF- You guessed it, golden rule numero uno! Slather that sunscreen on winter, spring summer and falll! you should be aiming for a 50++ strength and re-apply throughout the day. 03 Effective hyperpigmentation treatments Niacinamide- AKA Vitamin B3 is clinically proven to reduce appearance of hyperpigmentation and even skin tone. Retinol- Woohoo one of our all time favs at skin kind! This ingredient helps speed up natural skin cell turnover meaning it helps reduce those unwanted dark spots. YAY for retinol! Vitamin C- Yes yes, we already mentioned Vitamin C but its so good we had to mention it twice. This wonderous ingredient will fade hyperpigmentation marks and give you a more even toned complexion.

Enlarged Pores

If you have…visible pores on your face, you likely have Enlarged pores.

Pores are the small openings on the skin that pass sweat and sebum out to the surface.. Kinda cool right? Enlarged pores refer to the pores that are more visible to the naked eye. A surprising fact is that enlarged pores are quite often genetic, Thanks ma and pa. But there are also factors such as sebum secretion and skin elasticity that may make pores larger due to oil combining with skin debris and causing clogging. You can tackle enlarged pores by targeting their undelying causes- i.e., the increased sebum secretion. Lets debunk a myth here “pores open and close” no, no, no my skin enthusiast children, it is impossible for pores to open and close on their own accord. They may however, dialte due to skin debris build up.

01 What Causes Enlarged Pores? Excessive sebum secretion- Eww, anyone else with me here? I kid of course, this is a totally natural process that we all go through. Unfortunately though, on occasion we may produce to much sebum especially on the nose area thanks to the sebaceous glands that are beneath the skin in this area. Not removing Makeup-Uh-oh, I know we are all guilty of this fact, we had a long day and crashed, we got a little wild at the weekend and forgot to remove our makeup- it happens, no judgement here queens. But consistently not removing makeup ends in our pores becomming clogged and enlarged. Skin Elasticity- As we age collagen levels decrease resulting in pores losing their elasticity. What does that mean? It means elasticity gives our skin its plump healthy look, as this decreases the pores start to sag and engorge. 02 How can you prevent Enlarged pores? Do the basic routine- Cleanse, Tone and moisturize daily, Exfoliate 2-3 times weekly, and add a specific treatment to your routine i.e., anti-aging or anit-oil. Avoid certain products- Thick/greasy creams, bar soap, harsh scrubs, dehydrating home treatments.. just do not do it. Check your ingredients and look for products that target pore reduction. Apply SPF- Yes queen! again with the SPF. You should be aiming for a 50++ strength and re-apply throughout the day.. 03 Effective enlarged pore treatments Collagen- Your skin is hungry for the creme-de-la-creme of ingredients so feed it collagen, this will help with the skins natural collagen production and help to keep skin looking plump, bouncy and as fresh as the day you were born. Glycolic acid- It is not as scary as it seems, a gently glycolic acid aka AHA will remove the top layer of the skin whilst simultaneously resurfacing working to regenerate skin cells and clear out pores. Salicylic acid- Aka BHA will act as an exfoliant and help to reduce sebum production making them less likely to become clogged and dilated.

Ageing

If it's…starting to lose it's bounce, plumpness or pizazz you likely have Ageing skin.

It's no secret that your skin changes as you age. Whether you notice loss of elasticity or volume, a few fine lines here and there, or discoloration where you didn't use to have it, these changes can be a bit stressful as you try to navigate a brand new skincare routine. The products that worked for you in your early 20s may lose its efficacy even as early as a few years later, and you constantly have to adjust your regimen based on your skin's current needs, does this sound like you? Couldn’t you just cream at the ever depleting quality... Well yes but we don’t need to. There are certain remedies and preventative measures we can take. Its never to late ladies and gentlemen to care for your skin.

01 What Causes Ageing? Natural ageing of skin- Okay guys, there is no denying our skin is naturally going to age with time, we need to face it and be okay with it. Sun exposure- Remember the one billion times before we mentioned sunscreen?? This is a huge reason why sunscreen is so important. As we expose our skin to the sun and its ever encompassing UV rays we inadvertantly increase the ageing process. The UV rays increase the production of melanin which is a first defense but once that defence is exceeded our cells start killing themselves, mainly to prevent cancerous cells but a sad side effect is the ageing of the skin.. WEAR SUNSCREEN GUYS. 02 How can you prevent Ageing? Limit sun exposure- Self explanitory, once we over expose our skin it naturally reacts and speeds the ageing process. Quit smoking- Bad news if you’re a smoker, much like sun exposure smoking speeds up the ageing process, plus you are wasting money on ageing faster when you could be spending it on skin-care. Wash your face regularly- When you leave makeup on your face over night, your skin absorbs most of it. Since most cosmetics contain harsh chemicals, this contributes to the oxidative stress your skin faces. That’s why taking an extra three to five minutes to wash your face at night is never a waste of time. 03 Effective Ageing treatments Collagen- Your skin is hungry for the creme-de-la-creme of ingredients so feed it collagen, this will help with the skins natural collagen production and help to keep skin looking plump, bouncy and as fresh as the day you were born. Retinoids- Retinoids are considered to be the holy grail anti-aging ingredient, no joke. Retinoids are a vitamin A-derived molecule that work deeper in than collagen to help with collagen remodeling, as well as on the skin surface to increase skin cell turnover and improve discoloration and uneven skin tone. Sunscreen- No explanation needed here, i’m sure we’ve terrified you into incorporating sunscreen into your routine already. Peptoids- Ooh-la-la so fancy. These active ingredients are building blocks of protein and send signals to help build collagen and depending on the peptide, they can also send signals to improve moisturization in the skin.

Sensitivity

If it's…tight, flaky, or red, you likely have Sensitivity.

Sensitive skin is a condition that can be experienced by all the skin types- You may not show visible symptoms but instead notice how products feel on your skin.. “remembers the burning sensation of the pound shop charcoal face mask” Sensitivity can be just that, an uncomfortable sensation or it can be symptomatic (also known as hyper-sensitivity) resulting in rashes, redness, swelling and more. One thing, it can be hard to distinguish if you have dry skin or sensitive skin, as annoyingly the skin type and concern tend to co-exist unharmoneously meaning dry skin is often sensitive and sensitive skin is you guessed it often dry. Boo skin, just boo. Lets take a look at some causes and treatments.

01 What Causes Sensitivity? Allergies- When your skin has an allergic response, it means your immune system is in overdrive. When your skin comes into contact with an allergen, your body tries to protect itself by releasing T-cells. This process can lead to redness and itching. In these cases, if you have an allergic reaction pay attention to the ingredients in products, it will be a hit and miss whilst you try and work out where the reaction is coming from, you can try researching common allergies and start terminating these ingredients from your products. Dry skin- It turns out the dry winter air may be enough to cause your skin sensitivity. According to the American Academy of Dermatology Association, excessively dry skin can put you at risk for developing another skin condition due to the irritation and itching. Underlying medical conditions- Certain medications, especially ones that treat hypertension, can cause dehydration and thus dry, irritated skin. Hormonal changes, such as those during the menstrual cycle, can also affect the skin. 02 How can you prevent Sensitivity? Rule out certain ingredients- If you are having reactions symptomatic or not to certain ingredients opt for products that don’t carry these ingredients. As a rule, most sensitive skin cannot handle harsh fragrences that are in some products, opt for unscented. Hydration- When skin is deydrated it can be a gateway to sensitivity as the skin cannot protect itself, opt for a moisturizer that creates a protective barrier over your skin and use morning and night. 03 Effective Sensitivity treatments Hyalauronic acid- Acid on sensitive skin? Are you crazy? Well yes but this is perfectly logical. Hyaluronic acid is a key ingredient that is naturally produced by skin. It’s a humectant with amazing capabilities, it can hold around 50x its weight in water, so just imagine how much moisture it helps your skin retain! Vitamin E- Vitamin E is an antioxidant, meaning that it defends your cells from free radicals and helps prevent cell damage. It’s also super moisturising and has been known to aid wound healing so this is for sure an active ingredient your sensitive skin will love! Ceramides- Ceramides are lipids that are naturally found in the outermost layer of the skin i.e. the epidermis. They basically act as the ‘glue’ between skin cells, forming a tight seal and helping protect against external contaminants. Ceramides are great at providing moisture, reducing signs of ageing and restoring the skin barrier. It also improves skin tone, making it feel more firm and plump.

Texture

If it's…If it’s…bumpy to the touch or makeup doesn’t sit well , you likely have Textured skin

Even though perfectly smooth, poreless skin is flat-out impossible (your pores serve a very important purpose in keeping your skin moisturized, y'all!), you can get pretty dang close with the right skin texture products and treatments, but you've gotta find out what's causing the texture first. How much time do you have? No, seriously, bumps on your skin could be so many different things, but typically, as we age, skin cell turnover (the shedding of dead skin cells) slows down, which can increase dullness and uneven skin texture. Your skin texture could also be bumpy from psoriasis, contact dermatitis, keratosis pilaris, or even acne scars and pockmarks. And, last but not least, the source of your texture could be the sun—i.e., the culprit behind most skin concerns, and for good reason.

01 What Causes Texture? Sun Exposure (again..sigh)- You're already aware of how sun exposure can mess with your skin tone (melasma, dark spots, blah blah.), but the same can also be said about its effect on your skin's texture. UVB rays cause the premature death of living skin cells, which can lead to increased skin roughness, while UVA rays increase pigment production that'll give your skin an overall mottled texture and skin tone. YAY Dead Skin Cells-Uneven skin texture is commonly a result of excess dead skin cells that build up on the surface of the skin. This can make areas of the skin feel rough or bumpy to the touch and can also give the skin a dull appearance. 02 How can you prevent Texture? Stay hydrated- Drink plenty of water as the moisture helps to make skin look and feel smoother. Moisturized skin also tends to lessen the appearance of dullness, which is a common symptom of uneven texture. Sunscreen baby- We know this guys! Exfoliate- It’s important to wash away those dead skin cells x2-3 per week with an exfoliant, which can be done either with a sonic brush or an everyday washcloth. The key is to find an exfoliant that isn’t too harsh on your skin since abrasive exfoliants can cause micro-tears and excess oil production. 03 Effective Texture treatments Vitamin C- Vitamin C is i’m saying it- a must-have ingredient for protecting your skin against the free radicals in the environment that lead to uneven skin. It also boosts brightness whilst also reducing the appearance of fine lines. Hyaluronic Acid-Moisture will keep your skin looking even and whilst binding to collagen to replenish and strengthen the skin barrier and increase cellular turnover. This process helps remove dead, dehydrating skin cells more quickly to prevent clogged pores and dry, flaky patches while helping smooth out fine lines or wrinkles for firmer, more supple skin. Calendula Oil- extracted from marigold flowers, is an essential oil known for its anti-inflammatory and protective properties to treat common skin issues that cause textured skin. It contains antioxidant-rich flavonoids and saponins to boost your immune system, skin hydration, and stimulate collagen production. Collagen production increases cellular turnover to exfoliate any dry patches and cleanse pores for a more even skin texture. These anti-inflammatory benefits of calendula oil can reduce acne, allergic reactions, rashes, and symptoms associated with chronic skin conditions, like eczema and psoriasis. Calming these symptoms can help reduce any bumpy skin texture.
